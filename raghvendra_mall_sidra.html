Dr. Raghvendra Mall is a Senior Scientist at the Qatar Computing Research Institute (QCRI), HBKU, working at the intersection of machine learning and computational biology. He earned his PhD Summa Cum Laude from KU Leuven, Belgium, under Prof. Johan Suykens.

At St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, in the laboratory of Dr. Thirumala-Devi Kanneganti, he made key contributions to understanding PANoptosis — publishing studies in Cell, Science Immunology, and Nucleic Acids Research Cancer that identified NLRP12, ZBP1, and other innate immune sensors as therapeutic targets in cancer and infectious disease. At the Technology Innovation Institute (Abu Dhabi), he founded and directed the Bioinformatics team of 10+ scientists, secured AED 5.5 million in competitive grants, and established collaborations with Caltech and Burjeel Cancer Institute for AI-guided CAR-T cell therapeutics. At QCRI, across his current and earlier tenures, he has developed machine learning frameworks for cancer immune phenotyping, compound–viral protein activity prediction, and multi-cancer biomarker discovery, with results published in Nature Medicine, Briefings in Bioinformatics, and Bioinformatics.

Dr. Mall has authored over 90 peer-reviewed publications, holds 5 patents, and has secured grants exceeding AED 5.5 million. He is an IEEE Senior Member, named one of the IEEE Computer Society's Top 30 Early Career Professionals in 2024, a joint winner of the Anti-PD1 DREAM Challenge, and serves as Section Editor at the Journal of Translational Medicine.

Dr. Mall's research is centred on AI-driven multi-omics integration and its translational impact on human disease. By combining machine learning with transcriptomics, genomics, proteomics, and spatial omics, his group develops tools and biological insights that bridge discovery and clinical application across three themes:

  1. Multi-omics integration for disease mechanisms. Graph neural networks, contrastive learning, and network-based models applied to bulk/single-cell RNA-seq and spatial transcriptomics to decode cancer heterogeneity, PANoptosis signalling, and immune-cancer-microbiome interactions — with direct relevance to patient stratification and clinical trial design.
  2. Protein language models & AI-guided biologics design. Fine-tuning ESM2 and AlphaFold-derived architectures for peptide toxicity, peptide–HLA binding, B-cell epitope discovery, and protein crystallization, in active collaboration with Caltech and Burjeel Cancer Institute for anticancer therapeutics design.
  3. Drug discovery & precision medicine. Embedding-based and generative AI for compound–viral protein activity prediction, drug repurposing, and personalised treatment response in Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML).
